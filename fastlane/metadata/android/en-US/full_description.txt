Talking Clock speaks the time out loud so you don't have to remember to look
at it — built first for people with time blindness, ADHD, or memory issues
who lose track of time getting ready and end up running late.

<b>The speaking clock</b> announces the time at an interval you choose (every
15 seconds up to every hour), aligned to round times, with a safety auto-off.
Arming it takes two taps, and it keeps talking with the screen off.

<b>The talking timer</b> announces game-style milestones — "Five minutes
remaining… five, four, three, two, one — time's up!" — with three built-in
announcement styles.

<b>Talking alarms</b> can speak the time while ringing, and hand off to the
speaking clock when dismissed, so the clock keeps you on schedule while you
get ready.

<b>The stopwatch</b> counts up with laps, and can optionally speak the
elapsed time and lap times.

Also: quiet hours, a nightstand mode (dim red clock, screen stays on),
four speaking styles, adjustable rate and pitch, dark/light/AMOLED themes —
and voice packs: record the announcements in YOUR OWN voice (or a friend's)
and import them; the app stitches your clips together. No AI involved.

<b>Private by construction:</b> this app has no internet permission at all.
No ads, no trackers, no accounts, no analytics. On phones without a
text-to-speech engine (GrapheneOS, CalyxOS), it walks you through installing
a free, fully offline one.
